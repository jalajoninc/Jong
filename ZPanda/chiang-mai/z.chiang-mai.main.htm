The food culture of Chiang Mai

Chiang Mai is located in the mountainous region of northern Thailand, approximately 430 miles from the capital Bangkok. Northern Thailand is home to a specific cuisine that can hardly be found beyond its borders. The region's cuisine is characterised by the deliberate use of chilli, turmeric, lemongrass and coconut. The city is known for its profusion of restaurants serving street food. Khao soy is one of northern Thailand's most popular dishes consisting of boiled and fried noodles flavoured with yellow curry paste and coconut. Khao soy is typically prepared with chicken or beef. Other popular dishes include pad Thai and coconut pancakes.
